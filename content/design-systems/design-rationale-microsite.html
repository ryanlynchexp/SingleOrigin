Color Strategy

Gold, not blue.

Every fintech reaches for blue — it says "trust" and disappears into the crowd. BankIQ chose gold: wealth, precision, institutional weight. It's an uncontested position in the competitive landscape.

Why light-first? A light foundation provides immediate familiarity for banking professionals working in bright office environments, ensures maximum readability for document-heavy workflows, and aligns with accessibility best practices. Dark mode remains available as a toggle for extended analytical sessions where reduced eye strain benefits power users.

Why gold specifically? Gold signals importance across the full hierarchy: primary CTAs, key financial figures, card borders that distinguish elevated content, and status indicators. Five token intensities keep it controlled — from full gold on buttons to a 10% tint on highlighted card backgrounds. Scarce by design, so the eye always goes where gold goes.

Cards, not containers. Every data surface is a card with defined edges and shadow — never a flat region. Users can see where one context ends and another begins.

Shadow = importance. Higher elevation means higher shadow intensity. Modals and action panels literally sit above content, creating instinctive focus hierarchy.

Brighter = higher. Each elevation step lightens the surface slightly — in dark mode and light mode alike — so depth reads physically, not decoratively. Glass is a separate layer above this scale.

Shape Language

Soft, not playful.

Corner radius is a trust signal. Too sharp feels cold and technical. Too round feels consumer and casual. BankIQ sits in the refined middle — approachable enough to invite exploration, serious enough to handle six-figure decisions.

All strokes use 1px weight at varying opacity — never thicker. Thin strokes create structure without visual noise. The border recedes; the content speaks.

The positioning argument: Bloomberg and traditional finance use sharp corners — they signal "data terminal, not designed for you." Consumer fintechs like Cash App use full rounding — it says "friendly, maybe frivolous." BankIQ's 6–10px radius says: "We took the time to design this carefully, and we take your money just as seriously."

Translucency

Layered
focus.

Translucent panels keep the layer beneath visible while the foreground leads. Users drill into a modal, sheet, or filter and still see where they came from — context and focus in the same glance. Three tiers scale with how much attention the panel deserves.

The four ingredients:

background rgba white at 5–16% — color bleeds through
backdrop-filter blur + saturate — softens, keeps colors vivid
border gradient stroke — light top → dim bottom
inset shadow top highlight — sells the physical surface

UI Approach

Components carry
the strategy.

Buttons: Gold gradient = primary action. One per screen. Secondary + ghost stay neutral. The eye always knows where to go.

Cards: Subtle border at rest. Gold glow on hover. The interface responds to attention without cluttering the resting state.

AI surfaces: Indigo/violet gradient. If it's purple, AI made it. No exceptions, no blending.

Data tables: DM Mono for all numbers. Gold highlights key values. Subtle row borders — scannable without harsh zebra-striping.
