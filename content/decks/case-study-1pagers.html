Case Study 01 / Five

Care,
made coherent.

A regional hospital network running 12 facilities asked us to design and migrate to a FHIR-compliant EHR dashboard that finally fits the way clinicians actually work — not the way somebody once thought they should.

Healthcare EHR
Dashboard
01
The What

Twelve facilities. Eight systems per patient. One unified clinical view.

Clinicians were losing 40% of their day searching for data across fragmented platforms, switching systems eight times per patient visit, and dismissing alerts they could no longer trust. We led the research, the IA, and the redesign of a FHIR-first dashboard built to give that time back.

Fourteen months. Eight designers, four researchers. Contextual inquiry across every site, iterative prototyping with the people who'd actually use it, and an ML-ranked alert system that finally separates noise from signal.

Eight components that brought the dashboard back to clinical reality.

Case Study 02 / Five

One app.
Every city.

Passport acquired a competing parking app with 250K+ loyal users. Two products. Two identities. Our job: merge them without losing the people who made them worth acquiring.

Passport Parking
App Integration
02
The What

An acquisition that feels like an upgrade, not a rebrand.

68% of acquired-app users were openly anxious about losing features they loved. The two products were perceived very differently — one "premium and reliable," the other "functional but corporate." A bad merge would have churned a quarter-million users overnight.

Nine months. Six designers, three developers. 47 features unified, 156 brand touchpoints reconciled, and a phased migration that kept service continuous from day one of the rollout.

Case Study 03 / Five

QA
that ships.

CarMax was moving from Waterfall to Agile. QA was perceived as the bottleneck, not the partner. They asked us to independently assess readiness, name the pain, and chart the path forward.

CarMax Agile QA
Transformation
03
The What

Rigor without the gate. Speed without the regret.

QA had been the team you handed work to at the end. Agile asked it to be the team you build with from the start. We ran 32 stakeholder interviews, mapped current and future states, built personas grounded in real interview data, and delivered an impact-vs-effort roadmap leadership could actually act on.

Five months. Four researchers, two designers. Independent perspective, deliberately — so the recommendations were trusted across QA, engineering, and product alike.

Case Study 04 / Five

Stars,
stalled.

A behavioral teardown of the Starbucks loyalty journey — finding the points where members stop earning, stop redeeming, and stop coming back. Strategic recommendations to bring them back in.

Starbucks Loyalty
UX Research
04
The What

Loyalty isn't earned at signup. It's lost in the middle.

Starbucks asked for an independent UX research engagement on the loyalty app — usage patterns, friction points, behavioral drop-off, and the strategic moves that could lift retention. Mixed-methods, qualitative-heavy, with comparative analysis against category leaders.

Deliverables: a behavioral journey map, prioritized opportunity matrix, and strategic recommendations grounded in the actual data.

Case Study 05 / Five

When everything
else stops.

An enterprise-scale disaster recovery platform built for Walmart — designed to keep stores running when networks go dark, power fails, and supply chains buckle. Resilience as a product.

Walmart Disaster
Recovery Tool
05
The What

A platform built for the day the lights don't come back on.

Walmart needed a unified disaster recovery tool — region-wide visibility, store-level status, decision support for crisis managers, and operational continuity workflows that hold up under real failure conditions.

Multi-disciplinary engagement spanning research, IA, design, and engineering — designed for the field teams who use it under pressure, not the boardroom that signs off on it.
